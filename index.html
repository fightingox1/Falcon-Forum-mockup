Why should you build your own PC?

Why you should build your own PC.

By Tyler Spruill September 29th, 2020

You’re probably asking yourself what reason do you have for building a computer? Well, there are many reasons why you should build your very own computer instead of just buying one pre-built. It is a common misconception that building a computer is hard, complicated, and time-consuming however, this is not at all the case. In fact, a pc is actually relatively easy to build, more like a complicated lego set. Relatively speaking there are many reasons to build your own computer, for example, building a computer is way cheaper than buying a pre-built. Normally manufacturers like dell and hp add upwards of 300 dollars extra to the cost that it actually is to build the computer. You can build a good computer for just 300 dollars that a company like Dell or hp would probably sell for 500 or 600. Or on the other hand, instead of just saving money you can spend the same amount of money you would on a pre-built to get way better specs. From my experience building, a pc can also teach you a lot about computers, and when building your own pc you will feel a lot better than if you just bought a random pre-built. It will make you be more careful with your pc, and more. Overall building pcs is a great idea, you can learn a lot about computers, you can customize it however you want, and you can save so much money. Just like God builds each and every person different, as it says in Romans 12: 6 when it says “According to the grace given to us, we have different gifts: If prophecy, use it according to the proportion of one’s faith” you don’t want the same pc that probably hundreds of other people have, made in some random factory for some random person. You want a pc that you worked on, different from the rest, standing out from other pre-build computers. Just like God doesn’t want all the same people or clones just wandering the earth, why would you want just clones of other people’s computers?
